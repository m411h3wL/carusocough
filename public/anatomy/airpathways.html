Air Pathways

Diagram

  • Trachea (windpipe) and the two main bronchi leading into the lungs. This is the main airway carrying air from the lungs toward the voice.
  • Larynx (voice box). This contains the vocal folds, where airflow becomes sound.
  • Pharynx (throat). Think of it as the central junction where the airway connects to the nose and mouth.
  • Nasal cavity. Air (or sound, depending on the soft palate) can travel through this passage, contributing to nasal resonance.
  • Oral cavity (mouth). This is where the tongue, lips, jaw, and palate shape vowels and consonants before the sound exits.
  • Lungs (the source of the airflow).
  • Diaphragm, the muscle that generates the air pressure for singing.
