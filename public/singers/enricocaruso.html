Techniques & Exercises

CARUSO#1

Notes

From Salvatore Fucito & Barnet J. Beyer, "The Art of Singing and the Vocal Method of Enrico Caruso" — appendix to "Caruso: The Only Authorized Biography", ed. Pierre V. R. Key. Munich, 1924. Originally published in a Russian vocal pedagogy anthology.

"Caruso sang this — his favorite exercise — without the slightest tension and as slowly as possible."
— Salvatore Fucito & Barnet J. Beyer

These are coloratura agility exercises — ascending and descending scalar runs, broken arpeggios, rapid note groups, and legato slurs. The key principle: practice with zero tension, as slowly as possible. Caruso believed that if the voice remained completely free while moving slowly through these patterns, speed would emerge naturally. This aligns with modern motor learning — prioritize precise, relaxed movement before increasing tempo.
